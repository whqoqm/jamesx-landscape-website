About

The Photographer

I grew up in Langley, BC, and spent most of my childhood hiking the trails of the North Shore and camping along the coasts of Vancouver Island. The mountains were always there — patient, massive, indifferent to whether anyone was watching. That's what drew me in.

Photography started as a way to remember places I'd been, and slowly became the reason I kept going back. There's something about the pre-dawn drive, the cold, the waiting — that pulls you into a different relationship with a landscape than a casual visit ever could.

I shoot primarily with a Nikon Z 9 and a D810, and I try to keep my post-processing honest. I don't composite skies or add mist that wasn't there. What you see is what the light did.
